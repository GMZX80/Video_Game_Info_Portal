Talk overview

A public history of technology, teaching and practical collaboration.

This talk offers a historical account of how Newcastle University has contributed to the global video game industry through research, teaching, technology development, and practical industry collaboration.

It reflects on technological disruption, from graphics hardware and parallel computing to simulation, AI, real-time systems, and game engineering. It also shows how Newcastle’s work, collaborations, graduates, and research culture have helped shape both the video game industry and the wider digital economy.

Why this matters

The story is technical, human and regional.

01

Technology

Graphics, simulation, AI, parallel computing, game engines and real-time systems sit at the centre of modern games engineering.

02

People

Students, alumni, researchers and industry collaborators carry ideas between classrooms, laboratories, studios and global technology companies.

03

Place

Newcastle, the North East and the wider UK games story show how regional expertise becomes part of an international industry.

04

Impact

Games technology reaches beyond entertainment into the digital economy, health, education, simulation and industrial collaboration.
