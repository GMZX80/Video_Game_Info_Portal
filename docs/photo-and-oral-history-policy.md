# Photo And Oral-History Policy

Generated: 2026-06-18

## Photographs

Every public photograph must reference a media record containing:

- source;
- original context;
- photographer where known;
- rights holder;
- permission status;
- date;
- original caption;
- identified people;
- identification source;
- confidence;
- public status.

Permission to use a copy of a photograph does not by itself establish ownership of the photograph copyright.

Do not identify anyone through facial analysis.

## Group Photographs

Group photographs must provide:

- keyboard-accessible numbered annotations;
- a text list with the same information;
- `identity under review` where unresolved;
- no hover-only labels;
- no definitive public name unless supported by the required evidence.

The first narrative-foundation PR does not use `A Photograph from Blaydon` as the exemplar story because the current rights and identification records are not yet sufficient for that opening.

## Private Testimony

Private messages are research leads and, where appropriate, first-hand testimony. They are not public quotations unless a permission record explicitly says they may be quoted.

Do not publish:

- full private messages;
- private email or message metadata;
- private contact information;
- private Drive IDs;
- unpublished personal allegations;
- unapproved quotations.

## Oral-History Records

Structured oral-history records should support:

- participant;
- interviewer;
- date;
- consent;
- quotation permission;
- transcript status;
- redactions;
- source ID;
- public status.

Private testimony must not be included in public JSON unless explicitly approved.

## Consent States

Use these states:

- `research-lead-only`
- `summary-approved`
- `quotation-approved`
- `transcript-approved`
- `withdrawn`
- `unknown`

Only `summary-approved`, `quotation-approved` and `transcript-approved` may appear in public narrative outputs, and only according to the permission type.
