# Editorial Method

This repository is both a public history and a working research file. Phase 0 now separates evidence from presentation: factual public sections are rendered from JSON data, while research registers record source provenance and unresolved claims.

## Evidence Standard

Use the strongest available source for each claim:

- Official records, patents, company announcements and contemporary magazine material first.
- Museum collections, institutional histories and directly involved participant interviews next.
- Retrospective first-person accounts as testimony, not automatic fact.
- Incoming posters, Wikipedia-like summaries and unsourced lineage charts only as leads.

Public confidence labels are deliberately plain:

- Confirmed
- Well supported
- First-person recollection
- Approximate
- Disputed
- Open question

Avoid numeric confidence scores. They look more precise than the source base allows.

## Relationship Rules

Never draw a solid line unless the relationship is legal, formal or explicitly documented as a company event. Staff movement, founder heritage, freelance work and skills transfer are historically important, but they are not legal succession.

Line treatment:

- Solid: founded, renamed, acquired, dissolved, legal successor, label owned by, regional office of.
- Dashed: employee moved to, freelancer worked for, founder previously worked at, staff heritage, uncertain relationship.
- Publication treatment: published by, commissioned by, licensed by.

## Copyright And Privacy

Do not republish whole magazine articles, scans, manuals or image archives. Use short attributed excerpts only where necessary, and prefer paraphrase plus source links.

Do not expose private Google Drive links, identifiers or metadata. CSC3224 teaching documents are cited as Graham Morgan archive material, without private URLs.

Do not publish learner work, assessment material, private contact details, home addresses, gossip, unverified personal stories or automatic photo identifications.

## Supplied Timeline Handling

The supplied `HISTORY_TIMELINE.html` is preserved under `research/incoming/` as incoming evidence. It is not treated as proof. Decimal dates from that file are converted to approximate public dates such as `c. 1983`, `mid-1980s`, `by 1990`, or `date not yet confirmed`.

Wikipedia-style rows in the supplied poster remain discovery leads until replaced by stronger evidence.

## Geographic Scope

For this project, North East England includes Newcastle upon Tyne, Gateshead, Blaydon, Northumberland, Sunderland, County Durham, Chester-le-Street, Stockton, Middlesbrough and Teesside. Leeds, Sheffield, Manchester, Liverpool, Scotland and the Midlands are comparison regions, not North East England.
