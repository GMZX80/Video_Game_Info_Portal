# Oral History Audit

This folder separates testimony from established fact.

## Evidence Rules

- First-hand testimony is a statement by the named person in an interview, portfolio, article, email, recorded conversation or signed note.
- A database entry, magazine profile, archive catalogue or task brief may corroborate a claim, but it is not oral history unless the named person is the speaker or author.
- No claim from oral history should be promoted to public fact until it is either independently corroborated or visibly labelled as first-person recollection.
- No photograph identification is accepted from appearance alone. Use published captions, named witnesses or multiple independent corroborations.

## Verification Statuses

- Verified: primary evidence plus independent corroboration.
- Strongly supported: credible first-hand testimony and supporting external evidence.
- Probable: credible lead but incomplete corroboration.
- Unconfirmed: no adequate source yet.
- Disputed: incompatible source evidence exists.
