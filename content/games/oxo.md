OXO is not useful because it settles the first-video-game argument. It is useful because it shows why that argument is too narrow.

The Computer History Museum timeline identifies A. S. Douglas's 1952 OXO as an early graphical computer game on EDSAC. It belongs in a history of human-computer interaction and graphical play. It does not do the same historical work as a public exhibit, a raster television apparatus, an arcade cabinet or a commercial home console.

This page is the Tier 1 game exemplar because the record is clear enough to support a short story-led treatment. It still remains carefully bounded: OXO is an early computer game, not the whole beginning of an industry.
